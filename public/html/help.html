Basics

  1. Choose your point group in the list
  2. Choose your pointing vector
  3. Choose a reference axis (this correspond to the vertical polarization axis with an angle of 0°)
  4. Enter your polarizations
  5. Choose the orientation of your sample with respect to the reference axis (the reference is fixed, the polarizations move)
  6. Click on "Calculate"
  7. Choose another polarization if you want and press again 'Calculate'

NB :
Press 'Reset table' if you want to change point group or database!
You can save results in LaTeX format

Notations

Results are presented using Porto notation as a(bc)d with :

  1. a the incident light direction
  2. b the incident polarization
  3. c the scattered polarization
  4. d the scattered light direction
